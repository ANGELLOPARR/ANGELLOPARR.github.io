A little about me...

I am a student at California Polytechnic State University, San Luis Obispo. Boy that's a mouthful. I'm currently studying to get a B.S. in Computer Science, and I'm hoping to get a minor in Data Science and eventually go to graduate school for an M.S. or Ph.D. in Computer Science with an emphasis on Machine/Deep Learning, Data Science, and Computer Vision.

I have way too many hobbies than I have time for. Most days I'm just your normal day-to-day student. I often am in class or reading textbooks for my classes from around 8 am to 4pm, which is usually when I'll start getting all my homework done for the week. When I have some free time though, I enjoy coding, cooking, surfing, and running. It's nice when I have a gap in homework so I can continue working on a project I have or my web development course, and when I've been sitting and typing for a long while, I'll usually balance it out with a mile run and a gym session or a couple hours of surfing. Having been born and raised in Bakersield, California (2 hours away from anywhere fun and always scorching hot) living in San Luis Obispo is an amazing step up from my old lifestyle. On the weekends I like to relax, hang out with my buddies, eat good food, and prepare for the week so that I don't get slammed with homework and responsibilities all at once.

If you've actually made it this far into my "About Me" page, you are probably either genuinely interested in hearing my story, in which case I would like to extend a Thank You for the interest, or you are a potential employer scouting my profile, trying to measure my web development performance, in which case I would again like to extend a genuine Thank You for your interest. I hope you'll enjoy the product of my time and effort that is this website. I'm an ambitious, honest, dedicated, and eager student trying to get hands-on experience with anything in the tech / software world. Since my first Computer Science Class in high school I was hooked onto the world of software development and tech. Watching my personal role models like Elon Musk release technology like self-driving cars and seeing companies develop super-intelligent A.I. that can beat the best humans in games like Go, Chess, and Dota 2 further lured me into seeing all of the amazing possibilities that Computer Science gives to us. The ability to start with a blank .txt or .python or .java file and develop software that interacts with the outside world using just brainpower, will, and a keyboard is––I think––too often taken for granted, and I can't wait to see what I will accomplish in the future with my canvas that is my laptop, and my brush that is programming.
